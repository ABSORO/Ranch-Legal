# <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold; text-transform: uppercase;">Constitution of the State of Monroe</span>

## <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Preamble</span>

We, the people of the State of Monroe, grateful for our inalienable rights, civil, political, and religious liberties, and desiring to secure them to ourselves and perpetuate them to our posterity, do ordain and establish this Constitution.

---

## <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Chapter I - Declaration of Rights</span>

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article I: Acknowledgment of Federal Supremacy</span>

The State of Monroe shall be an inseparable part of the federal union, and the Constitution of the United States is the supreme law of the land.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article II: Equality for All</span>

In their inherent right to life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness, all members of the human race are equal, be they permanent or temporary residents of this State.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article III: Freedom of Speech</span>

Every person may freely speak, write, and publish on all subjects, being responsible for the abuse of that right; and in all trials for libel, both civil and criminal, the truth, when published with good intent and justifiable ends, shall be a sufficient defense.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article IV: Security Against Search and Seizure</span>

The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, and effects against unreasonable searches and seizures shall not be violated, and no warrant shall be issued but upon probable cause, supported by affidavit, particularly describing the place to be searched or the person or thing to be seized.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article V: Peaceful Assembly</span>

The right of petition, and of the people peaceably to assemble to consult for the common good, and to make known their opinions, shall never be denied or abridged.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article VI: Due Process</span>

No person shall be deprived of life, liberty, or property without due process of law.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article VII: Self Incrimination & Double Jeopardy</span>

No person shall be compelled to testify against themselves in any criminal case, nor shall any person be twice put in jeopardy for the same offense.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article VIII: Right to Bear Arms</span>

The people have the right to bear and maintain arms for the defense of themselves, their property, their rights, their liberties, and the defense of the State.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article IX: Bail & Punishment</span>

All persons shall be bailable by sufficient sureties, except for significant felony offenses when the proof is evident or the presumption great. Excessive bail shall not be required, nor excessive fines imposed, nor shall cruel or unusual punishment be inflicted.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article X: Habeas Corpus</span>

The privilege of the writ of habeas corpus shall not be suspended unless, in the case of rebellion or invasion, the public safety may require it.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article XI: Religious Freedom</span>

The free exercise and enjoyment of religious profession and worship without discrimination or preference shall be forever guaranteed in this state.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article XII: Freedom of Election</span>

Elections shall be open, free, and equal, and no power, civil or military, shall at any time interfere to prevent the exercise of the right of suffrage.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article XIII: Extradition Rights</span>

No resident of the State of Monroe may be extradited to another State or Nation unless a treaty has been signed clarifying the terms of such exchanges. On this basis, all residents enjoy safety from foreign incursions on their rights and liberties within the State.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article XIV: Freedom of Movement</span>

Residents of the State of Monroe retain all rights to move between counties of said State unless restricted by the courts of this State.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article XV: Hunting Rights</span>

The opportunity to fish, hunt, and trap wildlife is a heritage that shall forever be preserved to the individual citizens of the State, subject to regulation as prescribed by law and ordinances. This right does not create a right to trespass on private property, native reservations, or diminish other private rights.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Article XVI: Rights of the Criminally Accused</span>

All accused persons shall have the right to defend in person and by counsel, to demand the nature and cause of the accusation, to have a copy thereof, to be confronted with the witnesses against them, to a speedy and impartial trial, and to have a process by which they may attain evidence in their favor. All convicted persons shall retain the right to appeal.

## <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Chapter II - Government Organization</span>

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section I: On Government</span>

The powers of the government of this state shall be divided into three distinct branches: the Legislative, Executive, and Judicial. No person or collection of persons charged with the exercise of powers belonging to one of these branches shall exercise any powers belonging to either of the others, except as expressly directed or permitted by this Constitution.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section II: On the Branches</span>

- **Executive Branch**: Presided over by the Governor.
- **Legislative Branch**: Comprised of elected persons, presided over by the Lieutenant Governor within the State Assembly.
- **Judicial Branch**: Presided over by the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section III: On the Organization</span>

- **Executive Branch**: Includes all Departments of State.
- **Legislative Branch**: Consists of all State Assemblies.
- **Judicial Branch**: Encompasses all courts within the State.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section IV: On the Governor</span>

The Governor shall be the executive officer of the State, elected for a term of three years (90 days). The Governor presides over all executive matters of the State and is aided by a cabinet. The Governor may be impeached by the State Assembly for high crimes or misconduct while in office, with impeachment hearings conducted in the Supreme Court.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section V: Duties of the Governor</span>

The Governor is responsible for signing bills into law, overseeing law enforcement within the State, maintaining a cabinet of officials, and acting as the Commander-in-Chief of the State Militia. The Governor may also grant pardons, commute sentences, and reprieve incarcerated individuals.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section VI: Duties of the Lieutenant Governor</span>

The Lieutenant Governor is appointed to carry out executive directives, administer government offices, and act as a liaison between the Governorâ€™s Office and local representatives. The Lieutenant Governor may act in the Governor's stead if absent and preside over the State Assembly, casting tie-breaking votes when necessary.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section VII: Judicial Branch</span>

The Judicial power of the State shall be vested in a Supreme Court with jurisdiction in all civil and criminal matters. Its judges have the power to issue warrants, writs, and orders as necessary to uphold common law. A Chief Justice is appointed to oversee the staffing and operation of the Courts.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section VIII: The Attorney General</span>

An Attorney General is appointed to represent the State in criminal and civil matters, safeguard the freedoms of citizens, advise state officials, initiate criminal investigations, and investigate corruption. The Attorney General is empowered to offer immunity and pursue or decline criminal charges.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section IX: Duties of the State Assembly</span>

The State Assembly is responsible for proposing new legislation, voting on laws, and petitioning the Governor. They may initiate impeachment proceedings against government officers for high crimes or misconduct. Assembly members serve two-year terms (60 days) and are elected by popular vote.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section X: County Sheriffs</span>

A Sheriff is appointed in each County to maintain law and order, staff deputies, execute court orders, and mediate minor conflicts. Sheriffs and deputies are considered law enforcement officers empowered to make arrests, conduct investigations, and maintain jails.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section XI: United States Marshal Service</span>

The United States Marshal Service operates throughout the State, ensuring equitable enforcement of the law where no County Sheriff presides. Marshals are responsible for apprehending wanted persons, executing court orders, and protecting government officers.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section XII: Immunity for Officials</span>

State officials are protected from being named in suits brought against them by citizens unless evidence shows they caused harm by infringing upon constitutional rights. No person may bring suits against the State without its consent.

### <span style="font-family: 'Georgia', serif; font-weight: bold;">Section XIII: County and State Boundaries</span>

The State of Monroe shall be divided into three counties and one territory: **Lemoyne**, **New Hannover**, **West-Elizabeth**, and the **New Austin** territory, with boundaries outlined on the official map.
