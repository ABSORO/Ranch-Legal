State of Monroe Penal Code

Notes

  • HUT = Hold Until Trial, meaning that a Judge shall sentence or give a trial depending on the individual's plea.
  • If a trial is required, open an LFM ticket.
  • When Judges are in town, a person may plead to all charges listed, and the Judge will handle all sentencing or fine assignment related to any case.
  • Judges may deviate from sentences listed here but MUST respect the drive for story and must never impose capital punishment (CK) or life sentences without explicit Admin approval or consent from the opposing players.
  • Lawmen should feel comfortable charging criminals with the listed times and fines but are also encouraged to lower them if circumstances call for it. Use your judgment to foster good storytelling so that both sides enjoy the encounters.
  • If caught blatantly committing a crime, not guilty verdicts will not be entertained.
  • Starting at 401 Days, sentences can transition to Years (OOC Days). 401 Days = 1 Year, 501 Days = 2 Years, 601 Days = 3 Years... (1 OOC Day = 1440 Minutes). However, if a time is already in years, this does not apply (i.e., 1 Year = 1440 Days â‰  10 Years).
  • Persons may plead Not Guilty to 1st Degree Felonies or higher as well as any individual charges with a stated time of 1 Year or more. All other charges can be appealed after the fact or handled by a Judge on the scene.

Maximum Sentences

MAX OVERALL TIME: 7 Years (7 OOC Days)

MAX OVERALL FINE: $300

Capital Felony Charges

Category Charge Max Time Max Fine Definition Notes
P.C. 1101 Capital Murder HUT N/A Any person who uses any heinous, atrocious, or cruel act manifesting exceptional depravity in the commission of murder in the first or second degree, including the deliberate killing of law enforcement, government, or medical officials.
P.C. 1102 Cannibalism HUT N/A Any person who knowingly and willingly consumes human flesh or coerces another person to do so through deception, threats, or force.
P.C. 1103 Mayhem 2-3 Year(s) $300 Any violent behavior committed within the confines of a Town, City, or Native Reservation that deprives citizens of Monroe of life on a mass scale, or an act that can reasonably be expected to cause such an outcome. Must be authorized by one of: Chief Justice, Judge, Sheriff, Undersheriff, or Chief/Deputy Chief Marshal.
P.C. 1104 Insurrection 7 Years $300 Any attempt at a violent uprising against the government of the State of Monroe or the United States of America. Must be authorized by one of: Chief Justice, Judge, Sheriff, Undersheriff, or Chief/Deputy Chief Marshal.
P.C. 1105 Torture 3 Years N/A Intentional infliction of severe physical or mental pain or suffering upon another person within one's custody without lawful reason. Max time should be used for severe instances (e.g., loss of body part).

State Felony Charges - First Degree

Category Charge Max Time Max Fine Definition Notes
P.C. 2101 1st Degree Murder HUT N/A No person shall act in the unlawful killing of human beings with a premeditated design to effect the death of the person killed and shall be held equally liable if death occurs from any premeditated act. Not to be used for typical gunfights or ambushes. There should be real RP behind the incident to show true premeditation.
P.C. 2102 Attempted 1st Degree Murder 1 Year $200 No person shall attempt the act of unlawful killing of human beings with a premeditated design to effect the death of the person being attempted upon. For anything not provable as premeditated, use Aggravated Battery. Not to be used for typical gunfights or ambushes. There should be real RP behind the premeditation and attempt to kill.
P.C. 2103 Vandalism of a Native Site 120 Days $250 No person shall, without lawful reason, destroy, damage, or deface any sacred tribal lands or artifacts, or engage in conduct that would recklessly cause damage to any sacred tribal lands or artifacts. Not to be used for more typical robberies that happen to occur at Native Camps. This is reserved for deliberate vandalism of important Native sites.
P.C. 2104 Unlawful Escape 1 Year $200 Any individual, or group of individuals, who attempts or successfully frees someone from law enforcement custody without lawful authority is guilty of this crime. If found guilty of Unlawful Escape, you are automatically found guilty of all other charges levied against you. Escape from Sisika requires LFM approval via ticket.
P.C. 2105 Corruption HUT N/A No person shall utilize their office to acquire benefit, pervert the course of justice, or willfully deprive a person of their rights or engage in unlawful conduct while acting in an official duty. Corruption must be approved out of character via an LFM Ticket. Can potentially lead to a CK on your character.

State Felony Charges - Second Degree

Category Charge Max Time Max Fine Definition Notes
P.C. 2201 2nd Degree Murder 5 Years N/A No person shall act in the unlawful killing of human beings without premeditated design to effect the death of the person killed and shall be held equally liable if death occurs from any non-premeditated act.
P.C. 2202 Manslaughter 1 Year $100 Unintentionally causing the death of an individual through an act that was neither excusable nor justified without malicious aforethought.
P.C. 2203 Failure to Appear HUT N/A No person shall fail to appear in court on their scheduled court date. This will result in a bench warrant being issued by the presiding judge, and a default judgment being applied concerning the original criminal proceeding. No bail available.
P.C. 2204 Unsanctioned Dueling 1 Year $150 Any kind of mutual duel utilizing firearms or any kind of deadly weapon not sanctioned by a Judge, Sheriff, or Undersheriff that results in the death of one individual.
P.C. 2205 Aggravated Battery 180 Days $80 No person shall use lethal force against another human being, except in cases of self-defense or law enforcement duties. This charge will cover most acts of violence with weapons, including gunfights, stabbings, and other uses of lethal force.
P.C. 2206 Kidnapping or False Imprisonment 120 Days $100 Unlawfully seizing and moving a person by force, or seizing and detaining a person against his or her will with an intent to carry that person away at a later time. This doesn't apply when taking actions to help someone seeking medical attention.
P.C. 2207 Extortion 60 Days $80 No person shall, without lawful reason, by threat, accusations, or violence, induce any person to do anything but not limited to: make ransom payments, make any kind of monetary transfer, or give up any kind of property.
P.C. 2208 Unlawful Detainment 60 Days $50 No person shall unlawfully detain another individual against their will without legal authority.
P.C. 2209 Possession of Explosive Weapons 120 Days $80 Unlicensed possession of an explosive device such as a stick of dynamite or fire-bottle. Includes dynamite sticks and fire-bottles.
P.C. 2210 Bank Robbery 300 Days $125 Any person who robs or attempts to rob a financial institution. If charged with this, the individual is not to be charged for the taking of hostages or evading law enforcement as those are included in this charge. Proceeds of a bank robbery are subject to criminal seizure.
P.C. 2211 Corruption of Public Office HUT N/A No person holding public office shall engage in acts of corruption, including accepting bribes, abusing power, or engaging in actions that harm the integrity of the state. Must be approved via an LFM ticket and is subject to removal from office.
P.C. 2212 Bribery 90 Days $150 No person shall unlawfully offer, give, solicit, or receive anything of value to influence the actions of an individual holding public or legal duty. Charges may apply to both the person offering and the person receiving the bribe.

State Felony Charges - Third Degree

Category Charge Max Time Max Fine Definition Notes
P.C. 2301 Arson 300 Days $150 The act of knowingly setting fire to, or charring, another person's personal property without consent or with malicious intent. The building must be burnt to a point of requiring rebuilding.
P.C. 2302 Grand Theft 60 Days $200 No person shall dishonestly appropriate property, valued at or above $200, belonging to another with the intention of permanently or temporarily depriving the other of it.
P.C. 2303 Fraud 60 Days $60 No person shall devise any scheme or plot for the purposes of obtaining money, property, or any other thing of material value, by means of false pretenses, representation, or promises. Includes embezzlement.
P.C. 2304 Obstruction of Justice 60 Days $50 Unlawfully hindering the discovery, apprehension, conviction, defense, or punishment of anyone who has committed a crime or of evidence. This should be an independent act not covered by another charge.
P.C. 2305 Business Robbery 100 Days $70 Any person who robs or attempts to rob any business other than a financial institution. If charged with this, the individual is not to be charged for the taking of hostages or evading law enforcement as those are included in this charge.
